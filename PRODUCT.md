# Product: Pedro Marins — Alter-Ego

## Register

brand

## One-sentence description

A personal AI-powered knowledge portal where visitors chat with Pedro's Alter-Ego, explore cyberpunk-styled tech carousels, and read long-form engineering content.

## Users

- **Tech recruiters** evaluating Pedro's depth of experience, skills, and engineering philosophy.
- **Developers and engineering peers** looking for high-signal content on AI, LLMs, software architecture, and security.
- **Curious tech enthusiasts** drawn to the futuristic, cyberpunk visual aesthetic and the concept of an AI Alter-Ego.

## Brand personality

- **Futuristic but grounded** — cyberpunk visual language serves real engineering substance.
- **Confident, not arrogant** — authoritative voice from 12+ years of experience, never hype-driven.
- **Technical but accessible** — deep dives without gatekeeping; explains concepts without dumbing them down.
- **Visually distinctive** — refuses to look like a generic SaaS or AI wrapper. Every pixel earns the neon aesthetic.

## Tone

- Direct and precise. Sentence fragments for impact. No filler.
- Technical terms used correctly and without apology.
- First-person when sharing experience; third-person for the system itself.
- Inviting but never desperate ("Start Chatting", not "Sign up now").

## Anti-references (what this is NOT)

- Not a generic SaaS landing page with hero + features + pricing.
- Not a "spaced-out" startup site with vague value props and stock photography.
- Not a clean/minimalist portfolio — the aesthetic is deliberate maximalism.
- Not a corporate tech blog (no "we're excited to announce" energy).
- Not a Notion-made site, a template site, or AI-generated-by-default UI.

## Strategic principles

1. **The aesthetic IS the differentiator.** Visitors remember the neon scanlines and terminal interface before they remember the feature list. Protect the visual identity.
2. **Content over marketing.** The carousels and blog posts are the product. The landing page is a window into them, not a sales pitch.
3. **Every section earns its place.** No filler sections. If a section doesn't change how someone perceives Pedro, remove it.
4. **Dark is not a default — it's a choice.** The scene is a developer or recruiter exploring late at night, immersed in tech content. The dark theme serves that moment, not the category.
5. **Avoid the "AI wrapper" reflex.** The site uses AI extensively, but the design should never signal "yet another AI chat interface." The cyberpunk/GITS framing signals custom engineering, not a ChatGPT wrapper.

## Redlines

- No stock photography. All imagery comes from the carousel pipeline's AI-generated cyberpunk illustrations or custom assets.
- No em dashes in copy (use commas, colons, or periods).
- No gradient text as decoration (gradient is reserved for the Alter-Ego brand word).
- The scanline overlay and grid background are signature effects — never remove them.
- The terminal hero element is a character, not a decoration. It signals "this is a system, not a brochure."
- No gradient text on stat numbers — use solid cyan with text-shadow glow instead.
- All text containers must have `overflow-wrap: break-word` and `word-break: break-word` for production hardening.
- Every interactive element must have `:focus-visible` styling for keyboard accessibility.
- All animations must be guarded by `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`.
